Apply For A Permit

Since 14 March 2020, the Northern Land Council’s Executive Council has been monitoring Covid-19 risks on Aboriginal land and, in conjunction with Northern Territory and federal authorities, has been limiting non-essential travel. Currently, NLC is limiting applicants to Transit, Residential and Work applications. Work includes applications for essential services, media, research and in conjunction with a valid Land Use Agreement, such as tourism agreements.
Due to Covid-19, applications for recreation purposes are temporarily unavailable from this website. For special circumstances, please download the permit application pack from the NLC website. Once completed, submit the pack via the Contact Centre (see menu), or emails permits@nlc.org.au, or contact us directly on 1800 645 299.

The NLC’s permit application process has been amended to include:· Mandatory disclosures regarding travel outside of the Northern Territory, contact with Covid-19 cases and quarantine arrangements; and Additional permit conditions, including mandatory social distancing requirements to minimise exposure, and following an acceptable Covid-19 Safety Plan if you are working.
Each permit application will be assessed on a case by case basis and can take up to 10 business days, or longer if it is a complex application.

Visitor

Traditional Aboriginal owners have identified a small number of recreation areas on Aboriginal land that visitors can access. Each site will have rules on visitor numbers and length of stay. Some sites will have allowed activities, such as camping, fishing or boating.

Recreation

Traditional Aboriginal owners have identified a small number of recreation areas on Aboriginal land that visitors can access. Each site will have rules on visitor numbers and length of stay. Some sites will have allowed activities, such as camping, fishing or boating.

Residential

Residential permits allow permit holders to reside on Aboriginal land, usually in a community, but also in worker’s camps and commercial or staff accommodation.

Transit

Permit holders can access private roads during the specific dates listed on their permit for travel through Aboriginal land. Stopping is only permitted in specified locations along transit routes.

Work

Individuals can apply for work permits and applicants need to outline work locations, periods, and the purpose of their work.
